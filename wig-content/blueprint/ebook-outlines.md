## BOOK 1: Presence: The Art of Being Fully There ($12.99)

**Opening Hook:**
There is a man in almost every room who is not quite there. His body is present. He is nodding, responding, engaging. But something essential about him has been redirected — to a screen he's monitoring, to an anxiety he's managing, to an image of himself in the room that he is watching from outside. He is, technically, present. He is not, functionally, there.

I spent years being that man. This book is about what I found when I stopped.

- Presence as a practice, not a personality trait
- The physiological components: breath, body, nervous system

**Chapter 2 — The Noise That Prevents It**

- Internal distraction: the less obvious and more damaging one
- The monitoring mind: watching yourself perform instead of simply being
- Why men who are highly capable are often the least present

**Chapter 3 — Presence in Conversation**
- What it means to actually listen vs. preparing your response
- The moment a conversation becomes real vs. managed
- Eye contact, silence, and the non-verbal signals of presence
- How to re-enter a conversation when you've left it

**Chapter 4 — Presence Under Pressure**
- Why stress is the condition that most reveals a man's actual presence
- The choice available in the half-second between stimulus and response
- Staying located in yourself when the environment demands reaction
- Practical techniques that work under real pressure

**Chapter 5 — Presence with Women**
- What women actually notice and when they notice it
- Why intelligence and accomplishment don't substitute for presence
- The kind of attention that creates genuine connection
- What most men get wrong about being attractive

**Chapter 6 — Presence with Children**
- Why children are the most accurate presence detectors available
- What it costs a child when a parent is not really there
- The quality of attention that constitutes real fathering
- How to come home and actually arrive

**Chapter 7 — Presence in Your Own Life**
- The man who is highly present for others and absent from himself
- Making contact with your own experience
- The interior life that gets crowded out by performance
- Practices that restore access to yourself

**Chapter 8 — Building the Capacity**
- Presence as something cultivated over time
- The daily practices that strengthen the attention
- How to measure progress
- What a man's life looks like when presence is the foundation
