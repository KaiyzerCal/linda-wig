# The Crossing Blueprint: A Self-Guided Reentry Course
### By Bishop Watkins | The Crossing Agency
**Individual: $197 | With Workbook Bundle: $297**

---

> *This is not motivation. This is a curriculum. Work through it in order.*

---

## MODULE 1 — Where You Are
**Learning Objective:** Establish an honest baseline — legally, financially, physically, and relationally.

**Key Content:**
- How to read your supervision conditions without assumptions
- Assessing your actual financial starting point
- Mapping your support network honestly
- Identifying the gaps before they become crises

**Workbook Exercise:** Complete the Reentry Baseline Audit — a 4-section assessment that maps your current status across housing, income, ID, relationships, and mental health.

**Action Checklist:**
- [ ] List all supervision conditions with dates
- [ ] Write down your total available cash and resources
- [ ] Name three people you can actually call in an emergency
- [ ] Identify your single highest-risk area right now

---

## MODULE 2 — Paper: Identity Restoration
**Learning Objective:** Obtain all necessary identification documents in the correct sequence.

**Key Content:**
- The document sequence: why order matters
- State-by-state resources for birth certificates
- SSA process and common delays
- State ID, driver's license, and suspended license reinstatement
- Fee waiver programs and reentry organization assistance

**Workbook Exercise:** Complete the ID Restoration Tracker — a sequential checklist with status fields, cost tracking, and follow-up dates.

**Action Checklist:**
- [ ] Order birth certificate (or confirm you have it)
- [ ] Schedule SSA appointment
- [ ] Research DMV requirements and costs in your state
- [ ] Contact reentry org if you need fee assistance

---

## MODULE 3 — Housing: Finding It, Keeping It
**Learning Objective:** Secure stable housing within supervision requirements and build a path to independent living.

**Key Content:**
- Transitional housing: what to look for and what to avoid
- Family housing: the conversation you need to have
- Private landlord strategies with a record
- Section 8 and HUD eligibility — what's true, what's myth
- Building a rental history from zero

**Workbook Exercise:** Complete the Housing Timeline Map — plot your current housing situation against 30/60/90/180-day stability goals.

**Action Checklist:**
- [ ] Confirm housing for next 30 days
- [ ] Research Section 8 waitlist in your area and apply
- [ ] Have the direct conversation with your housing provider about expectations
- [ ] Identify one backup housing option

---

## MODULE 4 — Benefits: What You're Entitled To
**Learning Objective:** Apply for every benefit you qualify for within the first 30 days.

**Key Content:**
- SNAP: eligibility, application, and common denials
- Medicaid and mental health coverage
- SSI/SSDI for disability and mental health conditions
- Pell Grants and educational funding
- Veterans benefits
- How to appeal a denial

**Workbook Exercise:** Benefits Application Log — track each benefit applied for, date submitted, status, and follow-up required.

**Action Checklist:**
- [ ] Apply for SNAP within first week
- [ ] Apply for Medicaid
- [ ] Check SSI/SSDI eligibility if applicable
- [ ] If veteran, contact VA within first 30 days

---

## MODULE 5 — Employment: The Honest Approach
**Learning Objective:** Develop an employment strategy that accounts for your record honestly and positions you for sustainable work.

**Key Content:**
- Running your own background check
- Ban the Box laws and fair chance protections by state
- The three-sentence disclosure method
- Industries and companies that actually hire
- Day 1 expectations vs. six-month trajectory

**Workbook Exercise:** Employment Strategy Map — identify target industries, companies, certifications needed, and your personal disclosure script.

**Action Checklist:**
- [ ] Run your background check
- [ ] Research Ban the Box status in your state/city
- [ ] Write your three-sentence disclosure
- [ ] Identify three target employers

---

## MODULE 6 — Skills and Certifications
**Learning Objective:** Identify and pursue one certification or skill that materially improves employment options within 90 days.

**Key Content:**
- The fastest credentials with the highest return
- OSHA 10 and 30 certification pathways
- CDL training and requirements
- ServSafe and food service certifications
- CompTIA and IT pathways
- Workforce development funding and free programs

**Workbook Exercise:** Certification Decision Matrix — evaluate four certification options against cost, time to complete, local demand, and conviction-related barriers.

**Action Checklist:**
- [ ] Identify your target certification
- [ ] Find a funded or subsidized program in your area
- [ ] Set a start date
- [ ] Set a completion date

---

## MODULE 7 — Money: The First Year Framework
**Learning Objective:** Build a basic financial system that prevents crises and begins building stability.

**Key Content:**
- Opening a bank or credit union account with a record
- Building a basic budget on an entry-level income
- Child support, fines, and court debt — how to manage it
- Avoiding predatory financial products
- Credit rebuilding: the first steps

**Workbook Exercise:** Month-1 Budget Template — income vs. fixed expenses vs. variable expenses vs. obligations. Simple. Honest.

**Action Checklist:**
- [ ] Open a checking account or prepaid account
- [ ] Map all financial obligations (fines, child support, debt)
- [ ] Build a month-1 budget
- [ ] Identify one predatory financial product you're currently using and make a plan to exit it

---

## MODULE 8 — Legal: Understanding Your Obligations
**Learning Objective:** Know every legal obligation in your supervision and beyond — so violations happen by circumstances, not ignorance.

**Key Content:**
- Reading your supervision conditions without assumptions
- Travel restrictions, curfews, and prohibited associations
- Mandatory reporting requirements
- Fines and restitution: payment plan strategy
- Expungement and record sealing eligibility

**Workbook Exercise:** Legal Obligations Inventory — list every condition with its specific requirement, compliance method, and next due date.

**Action Checklist:**
- [ ] List all supervision conditions with specific requirements
- [ ] Know your next mandatory reporting date
- [ ] Research expungement eligibility in your state
- [ ] If behind on fines, contact the court about a payment plan

---

## MODULE 9 — Family: Coming Home to Real People
**Learning Objective:** Navigate family reentry with honesty, patience, and appropriate expectations.

**Key Content:**
- What incarceration did to your family (not what you think — what's actually true)
- The first conversation: with children, with partners, with parents
- Rebuilding trust through demonstrated consistency
- Co-parenting and legal parental rights
- When family is the risk, not the resource

**Workbook Exercise:** Family Relationship Audit — assess each key relationship for current status, what's needed, and a 90-day action plan.

**Action Checklist:**
- [ ] Have the direct family conversation (don't perform — talk)
- [ ] Identify which relationships need the most attention first
- [ ] Make one consistent commitment and keep it for 30 days before asking for anything
- [ ] If co-parenting, research your legal rights

---

## MODULE 10 — Mental Health: The Interior Work
**Learning Objective:** Identify the psychological effects of incarceration and build a sustainable mental health plan.

**Key Content:**
- Institutionalization and what it looks like on the outside
- PTSD, depression, and anxiety as reentry realities
- Identifying personal triggers and building response plans
- Accessing mental health treatment with limited income
- The difference between shame and guilt

**Workbook Exercise:** Trigger Map and Response Plan — identify your top five triggers, rate their intensity, and write a specific response for each.

**Action Checklist:**
- [ ] Connect with a counselor or peer support group
- [ ] Complete the Trigger Map
- [ ] Identify two people you can call before making a bad decision
- [ ] Schedule a mental health intake appointment

---

## MODULE 11 — Purpose: Building Toward Something
**Learning Objective:** Develop a 12-month vision that gives daily decisions a direction.

**Key Content:**
- The difference between surviving and building
- Defining what success looks like in your specific life
- How your experience becomes an asset, not just a liability
- Setting goals that are ambitious but grounded
- Building a daily practice that supports your direction

**Workbook Exercise:** 12-Month Blueprint — one page. Three goals across employment, housing, and relationships. What does each one require? What is the first step for each?

**Action Checklist:**
- [ ] Write your 12-month vision (one paragraph)
- [ ] Identify three concrete goals within it
- [ ] Identify the first action for each goal
- [ ] Share it with one person who will hold you to it

---

## MODULE 12 — The Long Game
**Learning Objective:** Understand that reentry is not an event but a process, and build the systems to sustain it.

**Key Content:**
- What the first year is supposed to look like vs. what it actually looks like
- How to handle setbacks without spiraling
- When to ask for help and how
- Building a community of accountability
- Using your story: peer support, mentorship, and advocacy
- What success looks like at Year 1, Year 3, Year 5

**Workbook Exercise:** Reentry Maintenance Plan — a monthly check-in template you use for the next 12 months to assess progress, adjust course, and celebrate real wins.

**Action Checklist:**
- [ ] Define what a bad month looks like and what you'll do about it
- [ ] Identify one accountability partner
- [ ] Schedule a 90-day check-in with yourself (calendar it now)
- [ ] Identify one way your experience could help someone else within the next year
