## Course Overview

Twelve modules built around the actual obstacles of the first year post-release. Not theory. Not inspiration. The practical framework, the honest assessment, and the sequential steps that move a person from walking out to standing up. Self-paced. Designed for someone who can give it 60–90 minutes per module.

**Who it's for:** People navigating reentry. Family members who want to support someone going through it. Caseworkers and counselors who want to deepen their toolkit. Formerly incarcerated people who have been out a while but feel stuck.

---

## MODULE 1: The Map Before the Walk

**Learning Objective:** Understand what the first year actually requires — not what the system says it requires — and build a personal navigation framework.

**Key Content:**
- The difference between compliance (what parole requires) and construction (what you need to build)
- The five domains that determine first-year outcomes: housing, documents, income, relationships, and internal architecture
- How to assess your starting position honestly without shame or inflation
- Why the first 72 hours set a tone that lasts for months
- Creating your personal first-year roadmap

**Workbook Exercise:** Complete the Starting Position Assessment — a structured self-inventory across all five domains. Identify your three most critical gaps.

**Action Checklist:**
- [ ] Complete Starting Position Assessment
- [ ] Identify top 3 priority gaps
- [ ] Write your 90-day goal in one clear sentence
- [ ] Schedule 60 minutes per week for course work

## MODULE 2: Documents and Identity Restoration

**Learning Objective:** Complete the full document restoration sequence and understand your legal identity in the system.

**Key Content:**
- The restoration sequence: Social Security card, birth certificate, state ID, voter registration
- State-by-state resources for each document type
- Handling suspended driver's licenses — hardship waivers and fee relief
- Correcting background check errors under the Fair Credit Reporting Act
- Your records: what to request, how to read them, what can be expunged

**Workbook Exercise:** Create your personal Document Status Tracker. List every document needed, current status, and next action step with deadline.

**Action Checklist:**
- [ ] Social Security card ordered or confirmed
- [ ] Birth certificate ordered or confirmed
- [ ] State ID application submitted
- [ ] Proof of address secured
- [ ] Background check reviewed for errors

## MODULE 3: Housing — Finding It and Keeping It

**Learning Objective:** Secure stable housing by understanding all available options and navigating the system with your rights intact.

**Key Content:**
- Understanding your parole/probation housing requirements in plain language
- Transitional housing: how to find programs, what to expect, how to use them strategically
- Private rental market: disclosure strategy, cover letters, landlord communication
- Your rights under HUD guidelines and fair chance housing laws
- What to do when family housing puts others at risk
- The 90-day plan to move from transitional to stable

**Workbook Exercise:** Build your personal Housing Options Map — list every available option in your area by category (transitional, shelter, private rental, family) with contact information and eligibility requirements.

**Action Checklist:**
- [ ] Confirmed current housing meets supervision requirements
- [ ] Identified 3 backup housing options
- [ ] Researched fair chance housing laws in your state
- [ ] Created 90-day housing stability plan

## MODULE 4: Income — The First 90 Days

**Learning Objective:** Generate income within the first two weeks while building toward sustainable employment.

**Key Content:**
- The income bridge: temp agencies, gig work, and day labor as starting points
- Second-chance employers and the Fair Chance Business Pledge
- The trades: why skilled labor is the fastest path to a livable wage
- Disclosure strategy: what to say, when to say it, how to practice it
- Ban the Box laws and how to use them without burning bridges
- Your rights when an employer uses your record to deny you

**Workbook Exercise:** Complete the Employment Readiness Assessment. Identify your transferable skills, certifications, and the disclosure narrative you'll use in interviews.

**Action Checklist:**
- [ ] Registered with 2 local temp agencies
- [ ] Identified 5 second-chance employers in your area
- [ ] Written and practiced disclosure statement
- [ ] Resume drafted (basic version)

## MODULE 5: Benefits and Financial Foundation

**Learning Objective:** Access every benefit you qualify for and begin building a financial foundation from zero.

**Key Content:**
- SNAP: eligibility, application, what to do if denied
- Medicaid and ACA marketplace coverage
- SSI/SSDI: who qualifies, how to apply, the appeals process
- Reentry-specific assistance programs: local funds, clothing assistance, phone assistance
- Opening a bank account with no credit history
- Understanding your credit report and beginning to build credit
- Avoiding predatory financial products — payday loans, check cashing, rent-to-own

**Workbook Exercise:** Complete the Benefits Eligibility Checklist and calculate your current monthly income gap — the difference between what's coming in and what stable life actually costs.

**Action Checklist:**
- [ ] SNAP application submitted
- [ ] Medicaid application submitted
- [ ] Bank account opened (or prepaid card secured as bridge)
- [ ] Credit report reviewed
- [ ] Monthly budget drafted

## MODULE 6: Supervision — Working the System Intelligently

**Learning Objective:** Navigate parole, probation, and supervision requirements in a way that keeps you out and moves you forward.

**Key Content:**
- Reading your conditions of supervision: what's mandatory, what's discretionary
- How to communicate with your PO — the approach that builds professional rapport
- What triggers violations and how to avoid them
- What to do if a violation is alleged — your rights, your options
- Travel restrictions and how to request modifications
- Early termination of supervision — when and how to pursue it

**Workbook Exercise:** Create your Supervision Compliance Calendar — all required check-ins, drug tests, and program participation for the next 90 days, mapped against your employment and family obligations.

**Action Checklist:**
- [ ] Read full conditions of supervision
- [ ] Scheduled all mandatory check-ins on calendar
- [ ] Identified 2 potential compliance conflicts and addressed them
- [ ] Established professional communication tone with PO

## MODULE 7: Family Reentry

**Learning Objective:** Approach relationship rebuilding with the framework that gives it the best chance — not the hope that love is enough by itself.

**Key Content:**
- What happened to your family while you were inside — the honest version
- The first conversation: showing up vs. explaining yourself
- Rebuilding with children: age-appropriate approaches, consistency, what they need to hear
- Rebuilding with partners: renegotiating roles, trust, and finances
- When family is connected to old patterns — the hardest navigation
- Building chosen family when biological family cannot anchor you

**Workbook Exercise:** Write the Family Inventory — for each key relationship, assess: current state, what they need from you, what you need from them, one action you will take this week.

**Action Checklist:**
- [ ] Identified 3 relationships to prioritize in the first 90 days
- [ ] Had one honest conversation with a key family member
- [ ] Set clear expectations with one person connected to old patterns
- [ ] Found at least one peer support community

## MODULE 8: The Mental Architecture of Reentry

**Learning Objective:** Understand what incarceration does to thought patterns and begin the deliberate work of internal recalibration.

**Key Content:**
- What hypervigilance, shrinkage, and the adaptation mind actually are
- Why the first 90 days feel disorienting even when everything is "going well"
- The pull of old patterns — why familiar feels like safe even when it isn't
- Shame vs. guilt: the distinction that determines whether you process or spiral
- Self-sabotage: what it looks like and how to interrupt it before it completes
- Daily practices that rewire the way the mind operates

**Workbook Exercise:** Write the Pattern Audit — identify your three most consistent thought patterns that served you inside and assess honestly whether each one helps or hurts you outside.

**Action Checklist:**
- [ ] Identified top 3 internal patterns to work on
- [ ] Established one daily mental health practice (journaling, meditation, exercise)
- [ ] Found one person to talk to honestly about internal experience
- [ ] Read The Mental Architecture of Freedom (Crossing Series Book 5)

- How to access mental health care with Medicaid, without insurance, and for free
- Peer support programs — AA, NA, and alternatives
- When a mental health professional is necessary vs. helpful vs. optional

**Learning Objective:** Understand the role that service and mentorship play in long-term stability — and find your specific way to contribute.

**Key Content:**

- Mentorship as the structure that holds you accountable in both directions
- Advocacy: how returning citizens are changing the system from the outside
- Speaking, writing, and community organizing as pathways
- The obligation to the person who is where you were — how to think about it

**Workbook Exercise:** Identify one person in your community you could meaningfully support right now — someone 6 months behind where you are. Write down one specific thing you could offer them.

**Action Checklist:**
