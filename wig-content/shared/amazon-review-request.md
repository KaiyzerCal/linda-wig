## HOW TO USE THESE

Send these as direct messages — text, DM, or email — to people you actually know. Not a broadcast, not a post. A real ask from a real person.

The goal is an honest review. Don't coach people on what to say. Just get them to the page and ask them to share what they actually thought. Honest reviews are more useful than five-star fluff — and Amazon's algorithm treats them the same.

Personalize the opening line with the person's name and a reference to how they know you or the book if possible.

## Notes for Calvin

**When to send the initial ask:**
- Send to people who have already read the book first — they're the easiest yes
- Second priority: people who know Bishop personally and would support the work
- Third priority: anyone who Bishop thinks would benefit from the book even if they haven't read it yet — offer a copy in the initial message

**When to send the follow-up:**
- 7 days after the initial message if no review appears
- Only send once. Don't send a second follow-up.

**How to check if a review was left:**
- Check the Amazon listing — reviews appear within 24–72 hours of submission
- Or simply ask them directly if you talk to them

**What makes a review useful:**
- Any review helps — even a one-liner
- Reviews that mention specific content ("the chapter on identity") are more useful than generic praise
- Star rating matters less than the existence of the review
